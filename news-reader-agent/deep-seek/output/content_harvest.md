The leaders of Cambodia and Thailand spoke by phone for the first time since a border conflict erupted in 2025, marking a step toward reconciliation. The phone call between Cambodian Prime Minister Hun Manet and his Thai counterpart, Anutin Charnvirakul, took place on Monday, June 22, lasting just over an hour. The call comes after a year-long border conflict that erupted in May 2025, leading to months of armed clashes, casualties, and the displacement of thousands of civilians. The UN-backed arbitration process launched earlier in June was also discussed, with both leaders expressing support for the maritime dispute resolution mechanism. The conflict began on May 28, 2025, when a fatal clash between soldiers occurred near the Preah Vihear temple area. The July 2025 ceasefire, brokered by ASEAN, was fragile and repeatedly broken, with renewed fighting in late 2025. A more stable ceasefire was achieved in January 2026, but tensions remained high. This phone call represents the highest-level direct communication between the two countries since the conflict began. Political analysts say the talks signal a potential shift toward diplomacy, though significant obstacles remain.
